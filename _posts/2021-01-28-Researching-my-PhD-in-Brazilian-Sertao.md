Hey everyone! Today I will describe one of my venting processes and how I managed to split up my time.
This week is the birthday of both my mother-in-law and father-in-law and we (I and my spouse) decided to travel and celebrate physically with them. After a long period in isolation, we scheduled our Covid-19 tests and started packing our baggage.

[Sertão](https://www.google.com/search?q=sert%C3%A3o&tbm=isch&ved=2ahUKEwid5LTi1r7uAhVpL7kGHa39Dg4Q2-cCegQIABAA&oq=sert%C3%A3o&gs_lcp=CgNpbWcQAzICCAAyAggAMgIIADICCAAyAggAMgIIADICCAAyAggAMgIIADICCAA6BAgAEENQ8AVYwAtgswxoAHAAeACAAbsBiAGsBpIBAzAuNZgBAKABAaoBC2d3cy13aXotaW1nwAEB&sclient=img&ei=mrQSYJ2vKene5OUPrfu7cA&bih=618&biw=1366) is a sub-region of the northeast region, and is somewhat similar to the "outback" in Australia. Going into a remote area has its priviliges, such as: the ability to get fresh air since there is no one close to me; reading my research papers outside, hearing the birds singing and petting cats and dogs; eating local delicies such as [oréia-de-véio](https://img.itdg.com.br/images/recipes/000/160/722/184471/184471_original.jpg) and alfenim. And, although this is a remote area, in my sogra's (mother-in-law in pt/br) house, we even have fiber connection! So, it's a win/win situation.

**Step 1**: Have your mendley up-to-date with downloaded papers.
I updated my entire mendley library and downloaded papers from downwards and upwards snowball so that I could go outside the house and still keep up with my related works research.

**Step 2**: Pack your devices.
My notebook already has a built-in camera, but I packed an USB camera anyway. In Sertão's houses, lighting conditions are a bit different, so I needed a high-res device. Packing everything is important since it is hard to get your hands on these devices around here.

**Step 3**: Predefine your tests.
I work with deep learning, and, as you may know, training models can be very timetaking. I connect remotely to my working server at the university and evaluate my models there. Even with fiber connection, it may be unstable sometimes. By predefining my tests, I can connect remotely even from my phone and execute the scripts that are ready.

**Step 4**: Organize your time.
Sertão is beautiful. There are a lot of native flora that keeps their appearance even after months without rain (see the following [website](https://www.todamateria.com.br/flora-da-caatinga/), in portuguese, but with a lot of nice pictures!). I organized my daily schedule to allow for nice, long walks in the Caatinga, following the trail right next to Pajeú River and taking pictures.

**Step 5**: Enjoy!
